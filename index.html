There are two ways to frame the GAN Generator loss,
and the non-saturating loss results in better training.

Minimax Loss L = log(1 − D(G(z))) original GAN

When the discriminator is confident the image is fake (left side of the chart), the curve is nearly flat. A flat curve means the gradient is close to zero, so the generator receives almost no learning signal and training stalls.

Non-Saturating Loss L = −log(D(G(z))) Goodfellow et al. 2014 ↗

By flipping the sign, the curve is steepest on the left, which is exactly where the generator needs help most. The gradient is large when the discriminator is most confident, giving the generator a strong push to improve.
